Chapter the first

About the author

in which we make her acquaintance

— portrait —

Our author, by her own account, was raised on circuit diagrams and library cards in equal measure. She trained first as an electrical engineer — a discipline that taught her to respect small voltages and smaller deadlines — and has since taken a long, roundabout path toward the question of why we build the things we build.

These days she keeps her attention on product: how it gets shaped, who it gets shaped for, and the strange gap between what people ask for and what they actually need. She teaches, occasionally. She writes, when the weather cooperates. And she remains, at heart, a maker of things that beep, blink, or otherwise prove they exist.

If you would like to know more, please turn the page.

Chapter the second

Projects & pet notions

small expeditions, mostly completed

What follows is a catalogue of things the author has made, studied, or otherwise tangled with. Some were assignments, some were obsessions, and a few fell between.

i. The Retrieval Dreams

A small system, built over several weekends, that tried to find things before they were quite lost. An experiment in retrieval-based tools and the particular joy of a good search result.

— retrieval · python · quiet weekends

ii. The Grant Hunt

An expedition, mapped carefully across many spreadsheets, into the unlikely wildernesses of community arts funding. Conducted on behalf of a theatre in a small New Hampshire town.

— research · community · pro bono

iii. Tea & Tradeoffs

An ongoing investigation into the small, quiet decisions that make products feel considered. Product thinking as practice, not performance.

— product · writing · ongoing

iv. The Preceptor's Toolkit

Teaching materials prepared for seminars on innovation and organizational culture. Discussion guides, cold-call prompts, and the occasional hand-drawn diagram.

— facilitation · teaching · design

v. Circuits & Civility

A hardware project from her electrical engineering days. Still on the shelf. Still, occasionally, beeping.

— hardware · nostalgia · unfinished

Chapter the fifth

Talks, teachings & tea

a brief account of seminars held and lessons learned

Teaching has always struck our author as the most reliable way to find out whether she actually understands something. Below, a short record of sessions facilitated and materials prepared.

i. Iconoclasts & innovation

A seminar session drawing on Gregory Berns's Iconoclast, with structured discussion prompts and a handful of gently-contentious questions. Materials distributed as a formatted guide.

— seminar · facilitation guide · pdf

ii. Organizational culture

A session built on Edgar Schein's Organizational Culture and Leadership. Includes cold-call prompts, a facilitation protocol, and notes on managing the inevitable silences.

— seminar · schein · facilitation

iii. Interview preparation, by request

Occasional one-on-one preparation for friends heading into product, engineering, or editorial interviews. STAR stories, behavioural questions, the usual. Available on request.

— coaching · on request · warmly
